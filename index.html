Part One

My Everyday Voice, Speech & Emotional Struggles

Real life. Real conversations. Real moments where your voice has held you back.

Confidence & Presence
I often feel nervous or anxious before speaking in a group, meeting, or in front of strangers — even when I know what I want to say.
My voice shakes or trembles when I'm under pressure, nervous, or trying to make a strong point.
I feel like I disappear in a room — people talk over me, ignore what I say, or don't take my words seriously.
I often second-guess myself mid-sentence and lose my train of thought completely.
I avoid speaking up in professional or social situations even when I have something valuable to say.
Clarity, Diction & Pronunciation
People frequently ask me to repeat myself because they couldn't hear or understand me clearly.
I know my pronunciation isn't sharp — I mumble, swallow words, or blur syllables when I speak fast.
I struggle with certain sounds or letters that I mispronounce or find difficult to articulate clearly.
My regional accent sometimes makes people misunderstand me or makes me feel judged professionally.
I speak too fast when anxious and can't control my pace, which makes me sound unclear or breathless.
Voice Quality & Tone
I feel my voice is too thin, nasal, or weak — it doesn't carry the weight or authority I wish it had.
My voice gets tired, hoarse, or strained after speaking for even short periods of time.
I breathe incorrectly while speaking — I run out of breath mid-sentence or gasp audibly.
My voice sounds flat and monotone — I know I don't convey warmth, enthusiasm, or energy naturally.
I hate the way my voice sounds on recordings — it embarrasses me and I avoid listening to myself.
Emotional Expression & Communication
I struggle to express emotions authentically through my voice — I sound either too robotic or over the top.
I find it hard to hold an audience's attention when I tell a story or explain something — people drift away.
I can't seem to modulate my tone effectively — everything comes out at the same emotional pitch.
In conflict or high-emotion situations, my voice either becomes aggressive or completely collapses — I can't find the middle ground.
I feel deeply disconnected from my own voice — like it doesn't represent who I truly am or want to be.
Professional & Social Impact
I've been overlooked for opportunities — promotions, leads, or roles — and I suspect my communication played a part.
I find it difficult to persuade, negotiate, or influence people even when my ideas are strong.
I freeze or go blank during presentations, interviews, or pitches — the pressure kills my performance.
My telephone or video call presence is weak — I sound unconfident or unclear on recordings and calls.
I use too many fillers — "um," "uh," "like," "you know" — and I can't seem to stop even when I try.
I've never received any structured training on voice, speech, or communication — I've just been hoping to improve on my own.

Part Two

My Voice Over & Dubbing Career Challenges

For aspiring and practising voice artists — from first attempts to professional barriers.

Beginner Level — Starting From Zero
I have no idea where to even begin — I don't know the difference between Voice Over, Dubbing, and other voice formats.
I've been told I have a "good voice" but have no idea how to turn that into a skill or a career.
I don't understand mic techniques, audio basics, or home studio setup — even the terminology is confusing to me.
I've tried reading scripts aloud but it sounds robotic and unnatural — like I'm clearly just reading.
I have no demo reel or portfolio and don't know how to create one that would actually get me noticed.
I don't know how to market myself or find clients, auditions, or projects in the voice industry.
Intermediate Level — Stuck Despite Trying
I can do basic reads but struggle to bring authentic emotion into corporate, commercial, or character scripts.
I lose control of my pacing — I either rush through scripts or drag them out unnaturally.
I cannot maintain consistent energy and tone across a long recording session — my performance degrades after 15–20 minutes.
My breath control during recording is poor — there are audible gasps, pops, or breath drops in my takes.
I struggle to take direction from a director and quickly adjust my performance — I freeze or resist change mid-session.
Switching between multiple character voices or styles is something I can't do convincingly — my range feels narrow.
I'm struggling to pass auditions even though I feel I'm good enough — I don't know what I'm doing wrong.
Dubbing-Specific Challenges
I have no training in lip-sync dubbing — I've never worked within the timing constraints of matching mouth movements.
I can't match the emotional arc of the original performance while also hitting technical dubbing cues — it's too much at once.
I struggle with translating scripts — ensuring the dubbed version sounds natural rather than like a direct translation.
I don't know how to adapt my voice to suit characters from different age groups, genders, or cultural backgrounds.
I've never worked in a professional dubbing studio and feel intimidated by the environment and workflow.
Advanced & Professional — Breaking the Ceiling
I've been doing voice work for a while but I feel stuck at the same level — growth has completely plateaued.
I still don't have a signature voice identity — I don't stand out or get recalled specifically for a genre or style.
My vocal health is suffering — I experience strain, fatigue, or recurring voice issues from heavy sessions.
I lack a strong professional network or agent representation and don't know how to access premium projects.
I haven't been able to build a sustainable income from voice work — projects are irregular and underpaid.
I've never received structured, expert mentoring — I've only learned by trial, error, and YouTube videos.

⚙️ Admin Setup Guide — How to Receive Lead Data

STEP 1 — Create your Google Sheet

Go to sheets.google.com → create a new sheet called Ronkel Voice Leads.
Add these headers in Row 1: Timestamp | Name | Phone | Email | Goal | Score | Band | Checked Items

STEP 2 — Create a Google Apps Script

In your Google Sheet → Extensions → Apps Script → paste this code:

function doPost(e) {
  var sheet = SpreadsheetApp.getActiveSpreadsheet().getActiveSheet();
  var data  = JSON.parse(e.postData.contents);
  sheet.appendRow([
    new Date(),
    data.name,
    data.phone,
    data.email,
    data.goal,
    data.score,
    data.band,
    data.checkedItems
  ]);
  return ContentService
    .createTextOutput(JSON.stringify({status:'ok'}))
    .setMimeType(ContentService.MimeType.JSON);
}

→ Click Deploy → New Deployment → Web App
→ Set Who has access to Anyone
→ Copy the Web App URL — you'll paste it in Step 3.

STEP 3 — Paste your details into this file

Find the line in the <script> at the bottom of this HTML that says:
const APPS_SCRIPT_URL = 'PASTE_YOUR_GOOGLE_APPS_SCRIPT_URL_HERE';
const SALES_WHATSAPP = 'PASTE_YOUR_WHATSAPP_NUMBER';
Replace the placeholder text with your actual URL and number (e.g. 919876543210 — country code, no + or spaces).
Then delete the yellow setup notice block from the HTML.

STEP 4 — Host & share the link

Upload this HTML file to any host (GitHub Pages is free). Every submission will:
✅ Save a new row in your Google Sheet instantly
✅ Open a pre-filled WhatsApp message to your sales number
✅ Show the lead a thank-you screen
